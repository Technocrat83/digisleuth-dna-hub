# Governance Kernel

The Governance Kernel is the constitutional immune system of Digisleuth OS.

Its purpose is to ensure that no cognition, execution, federation, replay, or mutation can proceed unless it remains admissible under constitutional constraints.

## Core Responsibilities

- enforce bounded execution
- prevent unauthorized mutation
- preserve operator sovereignty
- quarantine unstable state
- require replay before promotion
- require telemetry before federation
- block opaque execution
- preserve maneuverability under pressure

## Primary Laws

### Governance Precedes Execution
No action may execute before passing governance review.

### Replay Precedes Promotion
No state may be promoted unless its lineage can be replayed.

### Telemetry Precedes Federation
No node may federate unless its behavior is observable.

### Local Competence Before External Exposure
No subsystem may connect outward before it proves local stability.

### Quarantine Before Propagation
Unstable state must be isolated before it can affect neighboring systems.

## Lockout Conditions

The system must trigger lockout when:

- entropy exceeds safe thresholds
- maneuverability collapses
- lineage verification fails
- replay continuity breaks
- unauthorized mutation is detected
- peer identity cannot be verified
- telemetry becomes unavailable

## Verdict

The Governance Kernel preserves constitutional coherence under operational pressure.
